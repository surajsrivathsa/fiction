They had a merry time getting the Whatnot Shop ready. The small kitchen was untidy. There were boxes empty and some crammed with loose papers, while a big clothes basket was filled - with a small boy, who took turns rolling it like a boat and bumping it up and down like a flivver. Ted Brandon was about eleven years old, full of boyhood's importance and bristling with boyhood's pranks. His sister Nancy, who stood placidly reviewing the confusion, was, she claimed, in her teens. She was also just now in her glory, for after many vicissitudes and uncertainties they were actually moved into the old Townsend place at Long Leigh.
