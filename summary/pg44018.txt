All the mottoes annexed to the chapters of this work, have been selected from the Author's dramatic and other poetical works, not yet published. "No hut shelters Comala from the rain." A family of travelling vagrants were overtaken on the high road just leading out of Keswick, on the Penrith side, by a gentleman on horseback. He had observed the same group begging during the entertainments of the regatta which had concluded but the evening before.
