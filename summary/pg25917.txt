Perhaps it was Jack Barry's own fault that he had spent three weeks loafing
about Batavia without a job. Fat jobs were to be had, if a fellow persevered
and could grin at rebuffs; but when he discovered that shore jobs for sailors
were usually secured through the Consulate, and that his own country's
Consulate Service was limited, as service, to cocktails and financial reports
to Washington, he decided to avoid that combination
